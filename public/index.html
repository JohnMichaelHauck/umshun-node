Umshun

What is UmShun?

A Multiplayer Game

UmShun is a delightfully competitive multi-player filler-word counting game. Play against classmates and colleagues at lectures and presentations.

Scoring

Get the most points by being the first to press the correct button each time the speaker says “um”, “like”, “uh”, or other filler-word. You get one point for each other player who presses the same word within two seconds after you do - so be quick! Once you play a word, however, you are "locked out" of the game for two seconds, so be wise. The top 10 players nicknames will show up on the scoreboard below.

Warning

Be warned that UmShun causes irreverent giggling, and may get you kicked out of class or fired, so try extra hard to be considerate. Also, it will distract you from listening to the speaker's content.

Setting up the Game

Type in a nickname for yourself. No need to use your real name, but don't be crude.

Type in a nickname for the speaker. Everyone needs to use the same speaker nickname (upper/lower case does not matter). A longer nickname helps prevent others across the world from spoiling your fun.

Start with the nine words to play provided, but if your speaker frequently uses some other filler-word, feel free to replace one of the words with that one. Of course, the only way you can score from that word is if other players also play that word (upper/lower case does not matter).

Ending the game

Close this tab or refresh your browser to reset your participation in the game.
